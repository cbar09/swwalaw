Simonds, Winslow, Willis & Abbott, Professional Association, is located in Boston’s Financial District and is presently comprised of ten attorneys and three administrative staff.

Among the prominent practice areas of Simonds Winslow’s attorneys are estate planning and administration; commercial and residential real estate transactions and proceedings; business formation and transactions; litigation, trials and appeals; and taxation of individuals, businesses and estates.

The lawyers at Simonds Winslow combine a high degree of professional skill with a dedication to individualized client service. Simonds Winslow has been consistently given the highest “AV” rating by Martindale-Hubbell, the national lawyers directory, in its peer review of attorneys. We hope you will take the time to review our Attorney Profiles and our Areas of Practice, and contact us if we can be of assistance in meeting your legal needs.

While the attorneys at Simonds, Winslow, Willis & Abbott maintain independent practices, they routinely consult with one other concerning client matters, and will refer a case for handling by an associate where the subject matter is more appropriate to that associate’s expertise. In this manner, the attorneys at Simonds Winslow are able to offer their client’s personalized attention that is complemented by the collective legal experience of the office as a whole.

History
Simonds, Winslow, Willis & Abbott traces its roots to 1903 when Stanley Bolster joined the law office of his law school classmates William Vincent and Sydney Wrightington at 84 State Street in Boston. In 1935 the Association moved to its present location at 50 Congress Street. In 1963 Henry D. Winslow joined the Association, followed in 1969 by John L. Simonds. The office then operated under the name Bolster, Lane, Simonds, Wadsworth & Winslow. In 1978, the Association adopted its present name, Simonds, Winslow, Willis & Abbott. Mr. Winslow and Mr. Simonds both passed away in 1999.
